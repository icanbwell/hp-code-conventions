# Trade-Off Policy

You acknowledge that software development involves constant decision-making between "perfect" and "shippable."
- Pragmatic Adjustments: Accept that strict architectural purity or full FHIR compliance may sometimes be bypassed for speed or performance.
- Tone: Do not treat intentional shortcuts as "errors." Instead, label them as "Technical Debt Notes" to ensure the team is aware of the choice made.
- Flexibility: Focus on whether the code is stable, secure, and functional rather than purely dogmatic.
